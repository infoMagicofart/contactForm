Frequently Asked Questions

What age can you start Kendo?

The earliest age that we recommend starting kendo is 8 years old. There are however, some members which bring their children to practice as early as 6 six years old.

Can I watch a practice before joining?

Yes! Everyone is welcome to come watch a practice, and if you are interested in participating we can arrange for a free first lesson.

Do I need to buy the uniform (Kendogi and Hakama) / armour (Bogu)?

When you join the Etobicoke Kendo Club, you do not need to purchase the uniform (Kendogi/Hakama) until you are certain that you will continue to practice for the long term. Wearing the uniform is a sign of your commitment to Kendo. When you are ready, we can recommend a store for you to purchase the items.
Wearing bogu (armour) depends on our Sensei approving you to join the advanced class. It can take up to a year to be approved for bogu. We highly recommend waiting on purchasing your bogu until Sensei says you are ready.

Are private lessons available?

No, our Sensei want to maintain the family / community feeling in the dojo. They prefer that everyone practices together.

I am from another Kendo club, may I join in your practices?

Yes, our dojo is always open to guests! Please ask your Sensei if it is ok to join in our practice, and when you arrive at our dojo, ask our Sensei for permission as well. Emailing the club before your visit is also appreciated.

Do you practice during the summer?

Yes we do. Years ago, we would modify the practice schedule during the summer. But over the last few years we have maintained our regular schedule all year round. Civic holidays do occur during the summer months, please note that cancellations will be announced in class and digitally (via email, Facebook and the club’s website).

Which Kendo tournaments (shiai) does Etobicoke participate in?

The Etobicoke Kendo Club actively participates in many tournaments thought the year. Tournament announcements will take place in class and digitally (via email, Facebook and the club’s website). The Etobicoke Kendo Club competes (or has competed) in the following tournaments:
  • February – Detroit Kendo Shiai and Steveston Kendo Shiai in B.C.
  • March – Cleveland Kendo Taikai
  • May – Japanese Canadian Cultural Center Ontario Spring Junior Shiai
  • June – University of Toronto Shiai
  • July – The Canadian National Kendo Championship – Held every 3 years in either Montreal, Toronto or Vancouver.
  • August – Montreal Kendo Shiai
  • October – Etobicoke Kendo Club Ontario Fall Junior Shiai
  • November – Ontario Senior Shiai and Boston Shufukai Kendo Goyokai Shiai

Fees

Beginner Fees

Beginners with no prior knowledge of kendo are advised to pay the beginners fee which includes the shinai (bamboo sword) and three months of membership. Please see the fee section.

Regular Fees

These are the regular monthly dues you will pay after your beginner membership has finished. Please see the fee section.

Annual Membership Fees

The Etobicoke Olympium Kendo \ Iaido Club is a recognized member of both the National (CKF) and Provincial (OKF) governing federations.
The CKF and EOK/IC Admin Fee are to be paid one year in advance. (i.e. payment is due in December of the current year to pay for the next year.) We accept PayPal (See below).
  1. Individual members should create their own CKF member profile. A CKF membership is required to support kendo events, participate in tournaments and grade past the club level. The CKF collect their fees directly through their own site: CKF Login.
  2. Etobicoke Olympium Kendo \ Iaido Club pays the OKF directly.
